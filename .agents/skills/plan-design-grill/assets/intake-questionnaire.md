# Plan/Design Grill Intake Questionnaire

Use this questionnaire when the user says "grill me" or asks for plan/design stress-testing, but the proposal is still too vague to interrogate well.

Ask only the highest-signal questions first.
Do not dump the whole list unless the conversation truly needs it.

---

## Minimum required questions

These are the default first questions.

1. What plan or design do you want stress-tested?
2. What decision are you actually trying to make?
3. What constraints or non-negotiables apply?
4. What alternatives are currently on the table?
5. What would make this proposal fail badly?
6. What proof would make you confident enough to move forward?

If these six answers are clear enough, begin the grilling flow.

---

## Goal and decision framing

Ask when the plan is still described too abstractly.

- What problem are you trying to solve?
- What changes if this plan succeeds?
- What happens if you do nothing?
- Is the decision yes/no, option A vs B, sequence choice, or readiness check?

---

## Proposal snapshot

Ask when the current design or plan is not yet concrete.

- What is your current proposal in one or two sentences?
- What is the current state today?
- What is the proposed state after this change?
- Which part of the proposal feels least settled to you?

---

## Constraints and appetite

Ask when risk tolerance or hard limits are unclear.

- What must not break?
- What time, staffing, platform, policy, or compatibility limits apply?
- Are you optimizing for speed, safety, reversibility, or long-term cleanliness?
- How much uncertainty is acceptable before you act?

---

## Alternatives and doubt

Ask when the current path sounds under-compared.

- What other options have you considered?
- Why are you leaning toward the current one?
- What trade-off worries you most?
- What is the strongest argument against your current proposal?

---

## Proof and failure

Ask when the conversation needs sharper reality checks.

- What assumption is doing the most work in this plan?
- What evidence supports it today?
- What is the cheapest way to prove or disprove it?
- If this goes wrong, who notices first and how?

---

## Ownership and next step

Ask when the path to execution is still mushy.

- Who owns the decision?
- Who owns implementation?
- Who owns validation or sign-off?
- What is the next concrete step after this grilling session?

---

## Prioritization rule

When time is limited, ask in this order:

1. the real decision
2. the current proposal
3. hard constraints
4. biggest assumption
5. main failure mode
6. proof needed to proceed
